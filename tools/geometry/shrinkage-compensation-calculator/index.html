Shrinkage Compensation Calculator

Oversize a model from a measured linear shrinkage rate so the cooled part approaches its target dimension.

Result

—

How the calculation works

Designed dimension = target dimension ÷ (1 − shrinkage rate). Dividing, rather than simply adding the percentage, compensates against the oversized starting dimension.

How to use this result

Measure shrinkage using a representative calibration part printed in the same orientation, material, settings, and cooling conditions. X, Y, and Z may require different measured rates.

Example

For a 100 mm target with 0.6% measured shrinkage, design slightly above 100.6 mm because shrinkage applies to the larger starting size.
