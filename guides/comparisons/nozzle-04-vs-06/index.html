0.4 mm vs 0.6 mm Nozzle

A 0.4 mm nozzle is a flexible general baseline; a 0.6 mm nozzle often shifts the decision toward throughput and thicker extrusion paths.

Make the choice from the job

Choose from feature size and desired wall behavior, not speed alone. A larger nozzle changes line width, layer strategy, and flow demand together.

What people often miss

Printer condition, material formulation, model geometry, orientation, and post-processing can change the outcome. Treat a comparison or troubleshooting step as a decision framework, then validate it on a representative print.

Use the workbench for the numbers

These tools help turn the decision into a material, cost, dimension, or time check without pretending that one generic setting fits every machine.
