TPU Filament Guide

A practical selection and handling reference for makers—not a substitute for the technical data sheet of a specific filament or resin.

Where it fits

TPU Filament is a sensible starting point for feet, bumpers, grips, seals, and flexible protective parts.

Why makers choose it

Its practical appeal is elastic recovery and impact cushioning.

Trade-offs to plan for

slow, controlled feeding and careful retraction tuning.

Print, shrinkage, and moisture notes

Flexibility changes both feeding and part design. Dry filament and a constrained filament path help; use a geometry that can flex instead of expecting a thick block to behave like rubber.

Material names describe a family, not a guaranteed result. Formulation, color, reinforcement, annealing, orientation, and your printer can change behavior. Use the supplier technical data for critical tolerances, heat exposure, or safety requirements.
